What are they?

A sorting algorithm is a sequence of instructions to order, essentially non-order data structures as arrays, lists and sets, through comparisons or other methods.

Complexity

Simpler methods like Bubble sort are good at understanding the basic of sorting, but at the same time they are also inefficient in terms of time complexity, having to iterate N^2 times in both, worst and average case, to successfully sort the elements.

Advanced methods like Mergesort and Quicksort are the best ways to sort in terms of efficiency, they take only N(log N) iterations/recursions in the average case.

Mergesort has the advantage that their all cases are N(log N), that makes him a stable sort and a good option when there are too many elements. In the other hand, Quicksort, which is a double edge sword, has the advantage that all swaps are made in the input array i.e in-place algorithm. Also, Quicksort use a pivot, that is a choosen element - prefered as randomly generated - that serves to compare it with the others elements, positioning the elements smaller than pivot to the left side and the great ones to the right. It has an insufficient performance when the pivot is not good enough or the elements are already/nearly sorted, having a time complexity of N^2 in the worst case.

Which one should i use?

Well, initially you should not care about it, in most cases the language will implement an efficient and updated sort algorithm in its native code, but if not, you could search for a Quicksort variant or faster algorithms back then.
